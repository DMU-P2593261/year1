Animal Crossing

What Is Animal Crossing?

Animal Crossing is a social simulation game where players spend their time stranded on a remote island in the middle of nowhere. Players can interact with non-player-characters known as villagers and can collect resources to help decorate their islands and earn money.

New Horizons

Animal Crossing: New Horizons is the latest of the Animal Crossing games which was released on to the Nintendo Switch in late March 2020. The popularity of this game has surged rapidly as the country went deeper into lockdown and was forced to stay at home and not go outside.

How It All Started

The first Animal Crossing title was released on the gamecube concole in 2001. The game features a young character who starts their life in a small village. After realising that you have no where to live you end up doing tasks for Tom Nook who eventually gives you a house which you can continue to upgrade and expand.

Since this game was released it has had many million downloads and soon became a very popular concept and game among the world.

History Of The Game

Animal Crossing was originally released in Japan on the Nintendo 64 on April 14th 2001 and was later released the same year on GameCube. The Animal Crossing series is very well known for its use of the internal system clock and calender to simulate a real passage of time which is linked to the real world. A list of all Animal Crossing games can be found below.

  • Animal Crossing (2001)
  • Animal Crossing: Wild World (2005)
  • Animal Crossing: City Folk (2008)
  • Animal Crossing: New Leaf (2012)
  • Animal Crossing: New Horizons (2020)

Main Demographic

The main demographic of the game really just sits with anyone who likes Nintendo games or consoles in general. Although there is no set sex for the game to be aimed toward, the amount of female players is larger than the amount of males.
