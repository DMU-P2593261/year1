What Is Minecraft?

Minecraft is an open world sandbox game that allows users to create and explore as little or as
much as they want. Minecraft currently sits at the number one spot being the most played and
downloaded game of all time. With over 200 million copies sold worldwide and 126 million
monthly players it certainly is not a game that will not be easily forgotten.

Gamemodes and Playing Styles

  • Creative Mode
  • Survival Mode
  • Adventure Mode

Creative Mode

Creative mode is a gamemode within Minecraft that allows players to use an unlimited
amount of resources and build whatever they want whenever they want without any limitations
of health or having to survive in the open. Most people consider creative to be a waste
of time as it is "not the point of the game". However, some people have used creative mode
to build some extraordinary things. For example, someone has successfully made a discord
community of over 50,000 people and rebuilt (scaled to 1:1 block scale)
the entirety of the world in a flat open world environment.


Survival Mode

Survival mode is the complete opposite to creative mode as when you spawn in to the randomly
generated environment you have no resources, just your bare hands and the world around you.
The main objective of survival mode is essentially to survive for as long as possible while
completing achievements and exploring different areas of the game to discover new materials
and resources. In survival mode the end of the game is often associated with the end dimension
which is where the ender dragon lives. This is the end-game boss which only the most powerful
and skilled players can defeat. Once players have killed the dragon a reward in the
form of a dragon egg is obtained, and a portal is opened for them to travel back home safely.


Adventure Mode

Adventure mode is a unique mode primarily used by map makers. Some players like to
create mini-games and maps that can be uploaded and downloaded online. These maps could be
anything from an extremely difficult multiplayer labyrinth or a nerve wrecking horror map.
Either way all these games have one thing in common. And that is adventure mode.
The reason this mode is specifically chosen for these games and maps is due to its
unique restrictions. Unlike creative and survival, you cannot break or place blocks in
adventure mode. You are purely limited to walking around and interacting with the environment.
No editing to terrain or buildings can be made, therefore making it easier for mapmakers
to direct players through a certain path or prevent accidental breakages of redstone or command
blocks controlling the map they spent hours creating.


The Three Dimensions

  • The Overworld
  • The Nether
  • The End

Within Minecraft there are three different dimensions that you can play in. There is the
overworld which is the default area in which you spawn in, the Nether which is a hell like place
with a very dangerous environment and The End which is a desolate landscape with not much
to say for itself. Players must use portals to travel between these dimensions.

The Overworld

The overworld is the first dimension that you will spawn in when you very first create a world
in Minecraft. Within the overworld there are over 60 different biomes which contain a wide
variety of plant and animal life. The overworld is essentially your home world and is where you
will spend most of your time while playing and exploring. The overworld is
around 60 million by 60 million blocks in size and this is to a 1:1 metre scale. This means
that the Minecraft world is theoretically seven times larger than the surface of the Earth.
Once the player reaches the edge of the map a place known as the farlands come in to view.
This is where the game code cannot render any more infinite landscape and the environment
and gameplay to break down and corrupt. The landscape here looks rather distorted compared
to everywhere else as the game cannot process the amount of numbers and code.
